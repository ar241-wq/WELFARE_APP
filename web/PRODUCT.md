# Welfare Marketplace — Product Context

## Product Purpose
A three-sided marketplace that replaces generic benefit packages with personalized, credit-based perks. Employers fund monthly credit budgets; employees spend them across a catalogue of real providers; Life Moments trigger automatic care packages that make employees feel seen as humans, not headcount.

## Users
- **Employers / HR managers** — set budgets, approve requests, manage teams, review analytics. Use on desktop. Goal: reduce attrition, demonstrate care, track ROI on benefits spend.
- **Providers** — gyms, airlines, course platforms, meal kits. List perks, set credit pricing, scan QR codes to redeem, watch earnings grow.
- **Employees** — browse, request, redeem perks via the mobile app (out of scope for this web build).

## Register
- Public marketing pages: **brand**
- Employer dashboard + Provider portal: **product**

## Tone
Direct, warm when it matters. Interface language is confident, never apologetic. Empty states are invitations to act. Error messages explain what happened and what to do next.

## Brand
- Primary: #3D5AFE (deep indigo-blue — precise, trustworthy, not banking-navy)
- Care accent: #E8623D (warm coral — reserved exclusively for Life Moments and care contexts)
- Background: #F7F8FA (barely-there warm grey, not clinical white)
- Text: #15161A (near-black, not pure black)

## Anti-references
- No navy-and-gold fintech cliche
- No cream-and-terracotta HR-platform cliche
- No SaaS-cream-with-purple-gradient
- No "obviously AI made this" dashboard: no identical card grids, no hero-metric template, no side-stripe cards
- No over-animation — motion is purposeful and minimal

## Signature element
The Care Ribbon: a coral (#E8623D) horizontal sweep animation that fires when an employer approves a care package. It is the one emotional moment in the product. Everything else stays precise and quiet.
